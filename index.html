About Me

Hi, I'm Sulipsa — welcome to my little corner of the internet.

I'm finishing up my B.Tech in Computer Science at Seemanta Engineering College, and if you asked me two years ago what I'd be building today, I probably wouldn't have said "AI-powered farming assistants" or "cloud-hosted microservices platforms." But here we are — that's the fun part about this field, it keeps handing you new things to fall for.

Things properly clicked during my internship at Rooman Technologies, where I went from reading about the cloud to actually living in it — spinning up EC2 instances, wiring up S3 buckets, watching a deployment actually go live instead of just working on localhost. That feeling never really got old.

Since then I've been chasing that same feeling across every project — teaching a model to help farmers make sense of soil and weather data through Smart Farming Assistant in Python, hosting projects properly through CloudGadgets instead of letting them die in a repo, and stretching into a larger microservices build with NatureNexus along the way.

Python and the cloud are where I'm happiest — the model training, the deployment pipeline, the "why is this EC2 instance not talking to that bucket," all of it. Scroll on and I'll show you exactly what that's looked like.
